<article class="level6 chunking-small type-Section has-history" id="19.34.010" data-cite="EMC 19.34.010" data-short-cite="19.34.010" data-history="/EMC/19.34.010/history"><header class=" full-header" data-type="Section" data-search="/EMC/19.34.010/search" data-share="/EMC/19.34.010/share" data-levelnodes-target="header"><h6 class=" inner-header"><span class="num">19.34.010</span> <span class="name">Purpose and applicability.</span></h6></header><div class="track-indicator selected d-none">
	<span class="glyphicons glyphicons-check" aria-hidden="true"></span><span class="text">This section is included in your selections.</span>
</div>




<p>This chapter establishes the standards for the amount, location and <a class="def" href="19.04.020__c9b78e17817e2e9155021f0c78fc6ed2">development</a> of off-<a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> vehicle parking, standards for bicycle parking, standards for on-<a class="def" href="19.04.130__891ecc24c22755b1a631e08d7bcbc0e1">site</a> loading areas and standards for access to parking from city <a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">streets</a> and/or <a class="def" href="19.04.020__f3179e1d20b9236beb6f538f85ea3f8a">alleys</a>.</p>
<section class="subsec subsec1 chunking-none type-Subsec1" id="19.34.010(A)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.010(A)" data-short-cite="19.34.010(A)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">A.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Purpose.</span> </span>The purpose of this chapter is to ensure that parking and loading facilities contribute to a quality and healthy urban environment, encourage the <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> of shared parking to maximize the efficient <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> of limited urban space, reduce the number of vehicle miles traveled, reduce hazards to public safety, and manage on-<a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> parking to benefit all users.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec1 chunking-none type-Subsec1" id="19.34.010(B)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.010(B)" data-short-cite="19.34.010(B)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">B.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Where Off-Street Parking Requirements Apply.</span> </span>The standards of this chapter apply to all <a class="def" href="19.04.020__c9b78e17817e2e9155021f0c78fc6ed2">development</a> within the city of Everett, including any off-<a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> parking required by the city or put in for the convenience of property <a class="def" href="19.04.020__1972e7dc97e69659f01816cf395ca456">owners</a> or users.</p>

<section class="subsec subsec1 chunking-none type-Subsec1" id="19.34.010(C)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.010(C)" data-short-cite="19.34.010(C)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">C.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">City of Everett Design and Construction Standards and Specifications for Development.</span> </span>The City of Everett Design and Construction Standards and Specifications for <a class="def" href="19.04.020__c9b78e17817e2e9155021f0c78fc6ed2">Development</a>, hereinafter referred to as &#x201C;city design standards,&#x201D; contain detailed standards for parking <a class="def" href="19.04.030__e2888caa81e44b167d7b8b393589ee06">lot</a> design, surfacing, and <a class="def" href="19.04.030__6129d538b39455ef86065aa65618e33e">driveways</a>. <section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.070(D)(1)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.070(D)(1)" data-short-cite="19.34.070(D)(1)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">1.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Residential.</span> </span>For mixed-<a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> <a class="def" href="19.04.030__6875b3c9ced67b7881a1af1c98efabd1">buildings</a> with residential dwellings or <a class="def" href="19.04.050__59cccf67acd646b580eb92afd4ecd80e">sleeping units</a> where fifty percent or more of the <a class="def" href="19.04.040__29f579cbb9681065ca65d4796a1d7d1e">gross floor area</a> is dedicated to daytime, weekday nonresidential <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a>, it is reasonable to expect that there would be a substantial amount of vehicle parking left vacant during the hours when it is needed by residents. The residential off-<a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> vehicle parking requirement <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> be reduced by up to fifty percent.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.070(D)(2)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.070(D)(2)" data-short-cite="19.34.070(D)(2)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">2.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Daytime and Nighttime Commercial Uses.</span> </span>Up to fifty percent of the off-<a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> parking required by this chapter for primarily nighttime <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a> such as theaters, bowling <a class="def" href="19.04.020__f3179e1d20b9236beb6f538f85ea3f8a">alleys</a>, bars and restaurants <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> be supplied by parking serving primarily daytime <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a> such as banks, <a class="def" href="19.04.060__ea29338341d44efb4f5f38b6ddbd5a15">offices</a>, retail stores, personal service shops, and manufacturing and wholesale <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a>; alternatively, up to fifty percent of the off-<a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> parking required by this chapter for primarily daytime <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a> <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> be supplied by parking serving primarily nighttime <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a>.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.070(D)(3)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.070(D)(3)" data-short-cite="19.34.070(D)(3)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">3.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Mixed-Use Projects, General.</span> </span>If more than one type of land <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> (see Table 34-1) <a class="def" href="19.04.010">occupies</a> a single <a class="def" href="19.04.030__e8e5464a944d8085f5b4f8d9419cf73a">structure</a> or parcel of land, the total requirements for off-<a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> vehicle parking <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> be reduced by five percent of the sum of the requirements for all <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a>. This option can be <a class="def" href="19.04.010">used</a> in lieu of specifically calculating daytime, nighttime, and/or residential <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a> per subsections <a class="cite" href="19.34.070(D)(1)" title="19.34.070(D)(1)&#x2002;Residential">(D)(1)</a> and <a class="cite" href="19.34.070(D)(2)" title="19.34.070(D)(2)&#x2002;Daytime and Nighttime Commercial Uses">(D)(2)</a> of this section, and does not require a transportation demand management plan.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.070(D)(4)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.070(D)(4)" data-short-cite="19.34.070(D)(4)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">4.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Churches and Schools.</span> </span>Up to one hundred percent of the off-<a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> parking required by this chapter for a church or an auditorium incidental to a public or parochial school <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> be supplied by parking serving primarily nighttime <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a>, at the discretion of the <a class="def" href="19.04.020__bd211164397850d5e1613208425d0efd">planning director</a> in consultation with the <a class="def" href="19.04.020__a7ab84800f678f74eca124169725e7ee">city engineer</a>.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.070(D)(5)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.070(D)(5)" data-short-cite="19.34.070(D)(5)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">5.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Other.</span> </span>A <a class="def" href="19.04.020__c9b78e17817e2e9155021f0c78fc6ed2">development</a> <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> propose other shared vehicle parking proposals for approval of the <a class="def" href="19.04.020__bd211164397850d5e1613208425d0efd">planning director</a> and <a class="def" href="19.04.020__a7ab84800f678f74eca124169725e7ee">city engineer</a>. These other proposals <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> include provision for other forms of transport and mobility.</p>




</section>
</section>
<section class="subsec subsec1 chunking-none type-Subsec1" id="19.34.070(E)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.070(E)" data-short-cite="19.34.070(E)"><p class="header"><span class="num">E.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Requirements for Shared Off-Street Parking.</span> </p>



<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.070(E)(1)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.070(E)(1)" data-short-cite="19.34.070(E)(1)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">1.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">No Conflict.</span> </span>No substantial conflict between the operating hours of the <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a> for which joint <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> of vehicle parking is proposed is allowed.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.070(E)(2)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.070(E)(2)" data-short-cite="19.34.070(E)(2)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">2.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Assigned Stalls.</span> </span>Vehicle parking stalls that have been assigned full time to individual tenants or occupants <a class="def" href="19.04.010">shall</a> not be eligible for shared parking.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.070(E)(3)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.070(E)(3)" data-short-cite="19.34.070(E)(3)"><p class="header"><span class="num">3.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Distance.</span> </p>



<section class="subsec subsec3 chunking-none type-Subsec3" id="19.34.070(E)(3)(a)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.070(E)(3)(a)" data-short-cite="19.34.070(E)(3)(a)"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">a.&#x2002;</span></span>Off-<a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> vehicle parking facilities <a class="def" href="19.04.010">shall</a> be located within five hundred feet of the <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> which they are to serve, measured along the access route with a safe walking path.</p>

<section class="subsec subsec3 chunking-none type-Subsec3" id="19.34.080(A)(1)(d)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.080(A)(1)(d)" data-short-cite="19.34.080(A)(1)(d)"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">d.&#x2002;</span></span>A TDM plan is not required for single-, duplex- or triplex-<a class="def" href="19.04.050__966fc300b19a16ac907029d697d780b0">dwelling units</a>.</p>

A preliminary transportation demand management plan <a class="def" href="19.04.010">shall</a> be submitted before a <a class="def" href="19.04.030__6875b3c9ced67b7881a1af1c98efabd1">building</a> permit is approved. Transportation demand management plans <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> be written in two steps:</p>



<section class="subsec subsec3 chunking-none type-Subsec3" id="19.34.080(A)(2)(a)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.080(A)(2)(a)" data-short-cite="19.34.080(A)(2)(a)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">a.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Preliminary Plan.</span> </span>When a transportation demand management plan is required, a preliminary plan must be submitted along with the <a class="def" href="19.04.020__c9b78e17817e2e9155021f0c78fc6ed2">development</a> application. The plan should include the requirements outlined in subsections <a class="cite" href="19.34.080(B)" title="19.34.080(B)&#x2002;What Is Required in a Transportation Demand Management Plan">(B)</a>, <a class="cite" href="19.34.080(C)" title="19.34.080(C)&#x2002;Transportation Demand Management Strategies">(C)</a> and <a class="cite" href="19.34.080(D)" title="19.34.080(D)&#x2002;Transportation Demand Management Performance Standards">(D)</a> of this section. If a preliminary plan is submitted and approved by the <a class="def" href="19.04.020__a7ab84800f678f74eca124169725e7ee">city engineer</a> or designee, then a final plan is not required until a <a class="def" href="19.04.020__c38dfb1da4e5923b9d5dde0ebf70f213">certificate of occupancy</a> is requested.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec3 chunking-none type-Subsec3" id="19.34.080(A)(2)(b)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.080(A)(2)(b)" data-short-cite="19.34.080(A)(2)(b)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">b.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Final Plan.</span> </span>A final plan meeting the requirements outlined in subsections <a class="cite" href="19.34.080(B)" title="19.34.080(B)&#x2002;What Is Required in a Transportation Demand Management Plan">(B)</a>, <a class="cite" href="19.34.080(C)" title="19.34.080(C)&#x2002;Transportation Demand Management Strategies">(C)</a> and <a class="cite" href="19.34.080(D)" title="19.34.080(D)&#x2002;Transportation Demand Management Performance Standards">(D)</a> of this section is required before a <a class="def" href="19.04.020__c38dfb1da4e5923b9d5dde0ebf70f213">certificate of occupancy</a> <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> be granted. </section>
</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.080(A)(3)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.080(A)(3)" data-short-cite="19.34.080(A)(3)"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">3.&#x2002;</span></span>A <a class="def" href="19.04.030__6875b3c9ced67b7881a1af1c98efabd1">building</a> permit or land <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> approval <a class="def" href="19.04.010">shall</a> not be granted until a final transportation demand management plan meeting the requirements outlined in sections <a class="cite" href="19.34.080(B)" title="19.34.080(B)&#x2002;What Is Required in a Transportation Demand Management Plan">(B)</a>, <a class="cite" href="19.34.080(C)" title="19.34.080(C)&#x2002;Transportation Demand Management Strategies">(C)</a> and <a class="cite" href="19.34.080(D)" title="19.34.080(D)&#x2002;Transportation Demand Management Performance Standards">(D)</a> of this section is approved by the <a class="def" href="19.04.020__a7ab84800f678f74eca124169725e7ee">city engineer</a> or designee, and a covenant approved by the <a class="def" href="19.04.020__bd211164397850d5e1613208425d0efd">planning director</a> requiring compliance with the approved plan is recorded by the <a class="def" href="19.04.020__d30e5176357e29420736c4872099fbc5">applicant</a>. The covenant <a class="def" href="19.04.010">shall</a> include enforcement mechanism(s), which <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> include, but are not limited to, enforcement pursuant to Chapter <a class="cite" href="1.20" title="Ch. 1.20&#x2002;Uniform Enforcement Procedures">1.20</a> EMC, injunctive relief, monetary penalties, and loss of units available for rental.</p>

<section class="subsec subsec3 chunking-none type-Subsec3" id="19.34.080(B)(3)(a)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.080(B)(3)(a)" data-short-cite="19.34.080(B)(3)(a)"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">a.&#x2002;</span></span>The anticipated travel demand for the project.</p>

<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.120(B)(1)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.120(B)(1)" data-short-cite="19.34.120(B)(1)"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">1.&#x2002;</span></span>All parking areas <a class="def" href="19.04.010">shall</a> consist of durable surface materials approved by the <a class="def" href="19.04.020__bd211164397850d5e1613208425d0efd">planning director</a> and <a class="def" href="19.04.020__a7ab84800f678f74eca124169725e7ee">city engineer</a>. Depending upon <a class="def" href="19.04.130__891ecc24c22755b1a631e08d7bcbc0e1">site</a> and soil conditions, <a class="def" href="19.04.020__1fdf9570a74a78462bfb2947250baffd">low impact development (LID)</a> stormwater management facilities are encouraged, and <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> be required by the city&#x2019;s stormwater management manual.</p>



</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.120(B)(2)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.120(B)(2)" data-short-cite="19.34.120(B)(2)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">2.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Residential Exception.</span> </span>For residential <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a> with six or fewer dwellings on a property, nonrequired vehicle parking that is located outside of the front and <a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> side <a class="def" href="19.04.040__7cf5df3b7cffebbad7be2b619ad6964f">setbacks</a> areas <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> surface materials in accordance with city design standards including grass block pavers and reinforced grass paving systems; provided, however, that parking in the area between a <a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a>-facing <a class="def" href="19.04.030__d84a9cefcef12b0be99c98cdd1295a1a">facade</a> and the <a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> must be on a paved surface as allowed by EMC <a class="cite" href="19.34.110(C)" title="19.34.110(C)&#x2002;Driveways">19.34.110(C)</a>.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.120(B)(3)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.120(B)(3)" data-short-cite="19.34.120(B)(3)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">3.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Residential Exception.</span> </span>Existing, legally established vehicle <a class="def" href="19.04.020__0a7c047a165c45f14a15cccfd2ce337d">parking spaces</a> for residential development established prior to June 6, 2024, are not required to be resized or modified to meet current parking requirements, except for compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act.</p>




</section>

Parking of trucks or fleet vehicles is considered off-<a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> parking for the purposes of calculating the parking <a class="def" href="19.04.030__e2888caa81e44b167d7b8b393589ee06">lot</a> interior <a class="def" href="19.04.020__ba0b621c8a657fa899456385405a6a7b">landscaping</a> required by Chapter <a class="cite" href="19.35" title="Ch. 19.35&#x2002;Landscaping">19.35</a> EMC.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec1 chunking-none type-Subsec1" id="19.34.120(D)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.120(D)" data-short-cite="19.34.120(D)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">D.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Tandem and Lift Parking.</span> </span>For all <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a> of parking systems that lift or stack individual vehicles, each vehicle accommodated by the stacker counts as an individual <a class="def" href="19.04.020__0a7c047a165c45f14a15cccfd2ce337d">parking space</a>. Tandem parking, which means <a class="def" href="19.04.020__0a7c047a165c45f14a15cccfd2ce337d">parking spaces</a> in a series without independent access, <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> be <a class="def" href="19.04.010">used</a> for nonrequired parking. For required residential parking, each space must be no less than twenty feet in length and each <a class="def" href="19.04.050__966fc300b19a16ac907029d697d780b0">dwelling unit</a>&#x2019;s assigned vehicle <a class="def" href="19.04.020__0a7c047a165c45f14a15cccfd2ce337d">parking space</a> must have independent access. Neither tandem nor lift parking <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> be <a class="def" href="19.04.010">used</a> for required ADA parking stalls.</p>

The <a class="def" href="19.04.020__a7ab84800f678f74eca124169725e7ee">city engineer</a> is not authorized to allow the loading and/or unloading of trucks to occur on public <a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">streets</a> except on a nonrecurring basis as approved through the temporary <a class="def" href="19.04.020__4464a81a1583b427705846c35c5123bd">street</a> <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> permit process. Noise <a class="def" href="19.04.110__7711d033984773f8cf932f6c114f8f95">mitigation</a> measures <a class="def" href="19.04.010">may</a> include architectural or structural barriers, berms, walls or a restriction on the hours of operation, if necessary to meet the requirements of the city&#x2019;s noise ordinance.</p>

<section class="subsec subsec1 chunking-none type-Subsec1" id="19.34.160(A)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.160(A)" data-short-cite="19.34.160(A)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">A.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Purpose.</span> </span>Facilitate and encourage the <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> of bicycles and other micromobility to support affordable transportation options, reduce vehicle miles traveled, and make physical activity safe and accessible to Everett residents.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec1 chunking-none type-Subsec1" id="19.34.160(B)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.160(B)" data-short-cite="19.34.160(B)"><p class="header"><span class="num">B.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Applicability.</span> </p>



<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.160(B)(1)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.160(B)(1)" data-short-cite="19.34.160(B)(1)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">1.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">New Construction.</span> </span>The number of bicycle spaces and other facilities required by Table 34-3, <a class="def" href="19.04.080__9e2d62f851ce06fe7526e38aa7468eeb">Bicycle Facilities</a>, Required Counts, <a class="def" href="19.04.010">shall</a> be provided.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.160(B)(2)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.160(B)(2)" data-short-cite="19.34.160(B)(2)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">2.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Expansion.</span> </span>Bicycle spaces and facilities <a class="def" href="19.04.010">shall</a> be provided when increasing <a class="def" href="19.04.040__bb47723b09e4818be0378c67de9b5fe1">floor</a> area or other measure of <a class="def" href="19.04.080__9e2d62f851ce06fe7526e38aa7468eeb">bicycle facility</a> requirements by addition or <a class="def" href="19.04.110__721591b5a37ca631bdef7302692bccc4">alteration</a>; and</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.160(B)(3)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.160(B)(3)" data-short-cite="19.34.160(B)(3)"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">3.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Change of Use.</span> </span>Any change of <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> which requires more <a class="def" href="19.04.080__9e2d62f851ce06fe7526e38aa7468eeb">bicycle facilities</a> than the previous <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> <a class="def" href="19.04.010">shall</a> provide such <a class="def" href="19.04.080__9e2d62f851ce06fe7526e38aa7468eeb">bicycle facilities</a> in accordance with the current requirements minus the <a class="def" href="19.04.080__9e2d62f851ce06fe7526e38aa7468eeb">bicycle facilities</a> by which the previous <a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> was deficient, taking into account any exception or reduction under this chapter.</p>




</section>

</section>

<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="19.34.160(D)(1)" data-cite="EMC 19.34.160(D)(1)" data-short-cite="19.34.160(D)(1)"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">1.&#x2002;</span></span>Long-term bicycle spaces must be located on <a class="def" href="19.04.130__891ecc24c22755b1a631e08d7bcbc0e1">site</a> or, when being provided cooperatively with other <a class="def" href="19.04.020__c9b78e17817e2e9155021f0c78fc6ed2">development</a>(s), within three hundred feet of the subject <a class="def" href="19.04.130__891ecc24c22755b1a631e08d7bcbc0e1">site</a> measured by path of travel.</p>



</section>

The cover must be permanent, impervious, and must project out a minimum of two feet beyond the bicycle spaces, unless those spaces are also protected by an impervious wall. The shelter provided by bike lockers or bike hangers <a class="def" href="19.04.010">shall</a> be considered sufficient cover.</p>

<article class="level6 chunking-small type-Section has-history" id="19.34.170" data-cite="EMC 19.34.170" data-short-cite="19.34.170" data-history="/EMC/19.34.170/history"><header class=" full-header" data-type="Section" data-search="/EMC/19.34.170/search" data-share="/EMC/19.34.170/share" data-levelnodes-target="header"><h6 class=" inner-header"><span class="num">19.34.170</span> <span class="name">Bicycle facilities, required counts.</span></h6></header><div class="track-indicator selected d-none">
	<span class="glyphicons glyphicons-check" aria-hidden="true"></span><span class="text">This section is included in your selections.</span>
</div>




<p>Bicycle secure storage spaces are required for most <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a> to provide secure and convenient places to <a class="def" href="19.04.020__af1be713869b9ba6ac601c4d5916dabe">park</a> bicycles and other forms of micromobility devices. These regulations ensure adequate short- and long-term bicycle spaces based on the demand generated by different <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a>. Minimum <a class="def" href="19.04.080__9e2d62f851ce06fe7526e38aa7468eeb">bicycle facilities</a> acknowledge the usage rates for different <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a>, and that this will vary by geography, being higher in mixed-<a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> centers than in the rest of the city.</p>
<p>In the following table, the right column names the <a class="def" href="19.04.010">uses</a>, while the second, third, and fourth columns provide formulas for how many long-term (LT) and short-term (ST) spaces must be provided in the city generally, or in mixed-<a class="def" href="19.04.010">use</a> centers.</p>
