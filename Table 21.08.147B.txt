<article class="level6 chunking-small type-Section has-history" id="21.08.100" data-cite="RZC 21.08.100" data-short-cite="21.08.100" data-history="/RZC/21.08.100/history"><header class=" full-header" data-type="Section" data-search="/RZC/21.08.100/search" data-share="/RZC/21.08.100/share" data-levelnodes-target="header"><h6 class=" inner-header"><span class="num">21.08.100</span> <span class="name">Neighborhood and Mixed-Use Zones.</span></h6></header><div class="track-indicator selected d-none">
	<span class="glyphicons glyphicons-check" aria-hidden="true"></span><span class="text">This section is included in your selections.</span>
</div>




<section class="subsec subsec1 chunking-none type-Subsec1" id="21.08.100.A" data-cite="RZC 21.08.100.A." data-short-cite="21.08.100.A"><p class="header"><span class="num">A.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Purpose.</span> </p>



<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="21.08.100.A.1" data-cite="RZC 21.08.100.A..1" data-short-cite="21.08.100.A.1"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">1.&#x2002;</span></span>Implement the Redmond <a class="def" href="21.78.120__2e4be5605e94d5916abeb04536bd372f">Comprehensive Plan</a> vision for complete neighborhoods and vibrant <a class="def" href="21.78.220__e33f52939de0aac2f6e22cccb142eb51">mixed-use</a> zones that provide a variety of housing choices in low and middle densities and opportunities to get <a class="def" href="21.78.100__bf733d8a933c1601697f364223fc7ecb">access</a> to services in residential neighborhoods.</p>



</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="21.08.100.A.2" data-cite="RZC 21.08.100.A..2" data-short-cite="21.08.100.A.2"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">2.&#x2002;</span></span>Allow a mixture of residential and commercial uses along major corridors and transit routes outside of centers.</p>



</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="21.08.100.A.3" data-cite="RZC 21.08.100.A..3" data-short-cite="21.08.100.A.3"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">3.&#x2002;</span></span>Retain vital commercial services while areas are transitioning to <a class="def" href="21.78.220__e33f52939de0aac2f6e22cccb142eb51">mixed-use</a>.</p>



</section>
</section>
<section class="subsec subsec1 chunking-none type-Subsec1" id="21.08.100.B" data-cite="RZC 21.08.100.B." data-short-cite="21.08.100.B"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">B.&#x2002;</span></span>There are three neighborhood zones: Neighborhood Residential (NR), Neighborhood Multifamily (NMF), and Neighborhood <a class="def" href="21.78.220__e33f52939de0aac2f6e22cccb142eb51">Mixed-Use</a> (NMU). These zones allow for a variety of housing types and some commercial activity.</p>



<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="21.08.100.B.1" data-cite="RZC 21.08.100.B..1" data-short-cite="21.08.100.B.1"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">1.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Neighborhood Residential Purpose.</span> </span>The Neighborhood Residential zone provides for primarily residential neighborhoods with a variety of housing types that serve <a class="def" href="21.78.170__ea4be67641b70cfa3fec0f86ea7f239b">households</a> at a variety of income levels. The zone allows for different types of homes such as, but not limited to, detached single-<a class="def" href="21.78.150__cefc4f7cbc8c34762e0f76703e7e174e">family</a> homes, duplexes, triplexes, fourplexes, fiveplexes, sixplexes, <a class="def" href="21.78.290__2b14e420bd0fc04e09512809ba68cd4d">townhouses</a>, <a class="def" href="21.78.280__1c7bab5638f02abb75b7a1e53c4401f8">stacked flats</a>, <a class="def" href="21.78.120__7c2965e461172fafef0d1c6c8a31b34d">courtyard apartments</a>, and <a class="def" href="21.78.120__ed61955a63a010b462a72860587d54d5">cottage</a> housing. Some nonresidential uses are allowed to provide amenities to nearby <a class="def" href="21.78.270__48e13062c4d02689178daa454f4671cf">residential uses</a> and to support complete neighborhoods.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="21.08.100.B.2" data-cite="RZC 21.08.100.B..2" data-short-cite="21.08.100.B.2"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">2.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Neighborhood Multifamily Purpose.</span> </span>The Neighborhood Multifamily zone provides for multifamily residential neighborhoods on lands suitable for residential <a class="def" href="21.78.130__330f49df8243756a8a4dc7f7f7ee6dfe">development</a>. This designation provides for stable and attractive residential neighborhoods which have an urban character and a full range of public services and facilities. To complement the primarily residential nature of these zones and encourage complete neighborhoods, some nonresidential uses are allowed.</p>




</section>
<section class="subsec subsec2 chunking-none type-Subsec2" id="21.08.100.B.3" data-cite="RZC 21.08.100.B..3" data-short-cite="21.08.100.B.3"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">3.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Neighborhood Mixed-Use Purpose.</span> </span>The Neighborhood <a class="def" href="21.78.220__e33f52939de0aac2f6e22cccb142eb51">Mixed-Use</a> intent is to create complete neighborhoods. These are neighborhoods where most human needs are located within a comfortable walking distance. The zone allows for small commercial spaces such as corner stores and <a class="def" href="21.78.150__abf0177d311bbf5b0058d9249d9d7145">food and beverage</a>.</p>




</section>
</section>
<section class="subsec subsec1 chunking-none type-Subsec1" id="21.08.100.C" data-cite="RZC 21.08.100.C." data-short-cite="21.08.100.C"><p><span class="header"><span class="num">C.&#x2002;</span><span class="name">Mixed-Use Zones.</span> </span>There are two citywide <a class="def" href="21.78.220__e33f52939de0aac2f6e22cccb142eb51">mixed-use</a> zones: Corridor <a class="def" href="21.78.220__e33f52939de0aac2f6e22cccb142eb51">Mixed-Use</a> (CMU) and Urban <a class="def" href="21.78.220__e33f52939de0aac2f6e22cccb142eb51">Mixed-Use</a> (UMU). <p class="heading"><b>NOTES:</b></p>
<div class="note" id="21.08.200.B__1"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">1</span></span>The FAR <a class="def" href="21.78.220__195fbb57ffe7449796d23466085ce6d8">may</a> be calculated for the entire project then distributed across the <a class="def" href="21.78.280__a7d6475ec8993b7224d6facc8cb0ead6">site</a> throughout multiple <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">buildings</a> and phases provided the maximum FAR is not exceeded. Where publicly <a class="def" href="21.78.100__05cacb83177558542e367abd89434567">accessible</a> <a class="def" href="21.78.240__53e263073a3716cbca0f87ee0b41220b">open space</a> and amenities are provided on upper <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dfba89a600b608b2b724efe1f06f599a">stories</a> or rooftop, and spaces meet all requirements of RZC <a class="cite" href="21.36.400" title="21.36.400&#x2002;Design Requirements">21.36.400</a>, the FAR for those spaces <a class="def" href="21.78.220__195fbb57ffe7449796d23466085ce6d8">may</a> be excluded from maximum FAR calculations when spaces include <a class="def" href="21.78.100__bf733d8a933c1601697f364223fc7ecb">access</a> to adjacent outdoor spaces designed per RZC <a class="cite" href="21.36.400" title="21.36.400&#x2002;Design Requirements">21.36.400</a>, plaza and <a class="def" href="21.78.240__53e263073a3716cbca0f87ee0b41220b">open space</a> design criteria.</p>



</div>

Incentives are not available for properties that do not contain housing. See RZC Chapter <a class="cite" href="21.55" title="Ch. 21.55&#x2002;Development Incentive Program">21.55</a>.</p>



</div>
<div class="note" id="21.08.200.B__3"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">3</span></span>New <a class="def" href="21.78.130__330f49df8243756a8a4dc7f7f7ee6dfe">development</a> <a class="def" href="21.78.280__efd9f26ca2fe77e2532fb6cb8a4d94be">must</a> retain or replace existing commercial square footage (excluding hotel and lodging uses). <a class="def" href="21.78.130__330f49df8243756a8a4dc7f7f7ee6dfe">Development</a> <a class="def" href="21.78.220__195fbb57ffe7449796d23466085ce6d8">may</a> reallocate or modify areas allocated to commercial uses, subject to a minimum floor area and active pedestrian uses requirements to be negotiated as a condition to any <a class="def" href="21.78.130__330f49df8243756a8a4dc7f7f7ee6dfe">development</a> agreement.</p>

<div class="note" id="21.08.200.B__4"><p class="heading"><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">4</span></span>Exemptions from ground floor ceiling height requirements:</p>



<p>a. <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">Buildings</a> that do not contain nonresidential uses; and</p>
<p>b. <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">Buildings</a> where 100 percent of residential units are <a class="def" href="21.78.100__aaf02d6b568dbeaf4e7f2b7179220307">affordable housing units</a> meeting the affordability threshold of RZC Chapter <a class="cite" href="21.20" title="Ch. 21.20&#x2002;Affordable Housing">21.20</a>; and</p>
<p>c. Where <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">buildings</a> that are interior to the lot and not adjacent to a public parking garage, an urban pathway or other pedestrian-oriented sidewalks or pathways; and</p>
<p>d. Parcels zoned UMU within the Marymoor Village Center <a class="def" href="21.78.280__efd9f26ca2fe77e2532fb6cb8a4d94be">shall</a> have a minimum 16-foot ground floor ceiling height to be consistent with the adjacent Marymoor Core zone.</p>
</div>
<div class="note" id="21.08.200.B__5"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">5</span></span>A minimum of 50 percent of the first floor nonresidential space <a class="def" href="21.78.280__efd9f26ca2fe77e2532fb6cb8a4d94be">must</a> meet the minimum ceiling height. First floor nonresidential spaces <a class="def" href="21.78.280__efd9f26ca2fe77e2532fb6cb8a4d94be">shall</a> have a ceiling height of 16 feet minimum for the entire first floor.</p>



<p>a. Where first floor ceiling height varies, the tallest ceiling heights <a class="def" href="21.78.280__efd9f26ca2fe77e2532fb6cb8a4d94be">shall</a> be provided on <a class="def" href="21.78.280__fe42e9297a9a0c44abd0adc9f03a45b6">street frontage</a>, spaces fronting urban pathways and/or mid-<a class="def" href="21.78.110__e1e4c8c9ccd9fc39c391da4bcd093fb2">block</a> connections, and in parking garage loading/unloading and waste pickup areas.</p>
<p>b. For portions of the <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">building</a> that have a first floor ceiling height greater than 10 feet to meet minimum ground floor ceiling height requirements, the additional ground floor ceiling height <a class="def" href="21.78.220__195fbb57ffe7449796d23466085ce6d8">may</a> increase the maximum <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">building</a> height. For example, if first floor is 20 feet, the maximum <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">building</a> height increases by 10 feet (20 - 10 = 10 foot increase in max <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">building</a> height).</p>
</div>
<div class="note" id="21.08.200.B__6"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">6</span></span><b>Properties in the CARA</b></p>



<p>a. In the <a class="def" href="21.78.120__3021814dd105bc4bc69523b219a0c6ba">critical aquifer recharge area</a> (CARA), <a class="def" href="21.78.120__2a0d30cb1153031c3dfc239c6e0407ea">construction</a> methods for <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">building</a> foundation support will be limited to types that can be <a class="def" href="21.78.120__2a0d30cb1153031c3dfc239c6e0407ea">constructed</a> to meet temporary <a class="def" href="21.78.120__2a0d30cb1153031c3dfc239c6e0407ea">construction</a> dewatering (TCD) requirements of RMC Chapter <a class="cite ctx-RMC" href="/RMC/13.25" title="Ch. 13.25&#x2002;Temporary Construction Dewatering">13.25</a>.</p>
<p class="heading">b. Where <a class="def" href="21.78.280__39c6ab51c6a55437020795fc6212e5b9">underground parking</a> is not possible due to RMC Chapter <a class="cite ctx-RMC" href="/RMC/13.25" title="Ch. 13.25&#x2002;Temporary Construction Dewatering">13.25</a>&#x2019;s TCD limitations, the maximum <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">building</a> height <a class="def" href="21.78.220__195fbb57ffe7449796d23466085ce6d8">may</a> be exceeded by up to 12 feet if all of the following criteria are met:</p>
<p>i. The additional height is the result of <a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">building</a> a maximum of one level of additional above-<a class="def" href="21.78.160__4b3b9db8c9784468094acde0f8bf7071">grade</a> parking; and</p>
<p>ii. Proposed parking added by the additional height <a class="def" href="21.78.280__efd9f26ca2fe77e2532fb6cb8a4d94be">shall</a> not exceed the maximum fully dedicated parking ratios in RZC Chapter <a class="cite" href="21.40" title="Ch. 21.40&#x2002;Parking Standards">21.40</a>; and</p>
<p>iii. The additional height on <a class="def" href="21.78.280__fe42e9297a9a0c44abd0adc9f03a45b6">street frontages</a> meets a 20 foot step-back to limit impacts to the pedestrian realm.</p>
</div>
<div class="note" id="21.08.200.B__7"><p><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">7</span></span>Other regulations will impact the <a class="def" href="21.78.180__23ecf4bb91cd9ca50416053e8b659f75">impervious surface area</a> and <a class="def" href="21.78.220__195fbb57ffe7449796d23466085ce6d8">may</a> result in less than the maximum impervious area. These include but are not limited to <a class="def" href="21.78.120__6b80bb7747129f66efc03530da19b543">critical areas</a> requirements, <a class="def" href="21.78.240__53e263073a3716cbca0f87ee0b41220b">open space</a> and landscaping requirements, parking, mid-<a class="def" href="21.78.110__e1e4c8c9ccd9fc39c391da4bcd093fb2">block</a> connections, <a class="def" href="21.78.300__ceba282b7418b7f199798b645e1cba56">utility</a> easements, and <a class="def" href="21.78.280__0ed537c5b4390c7a581135f79175a035">stormwater management</a>.</p>



</div>
<div class="note" id="21.08.200.B__8"><p class="heading"><span class="header num-only"><span class="num">8</span></span><a class="def" href="21.78.210__c9151b5f2819a9be2b150813e4befe82">Lot coverage</a>&#x202F;for&#x202F;<a class="def" href="21.78.280__dc4c71563b9bc39a65be853457e6b7b6">structures</a>&#x202F;maximum will be 60 percent of total area for lots which meet the following requirements:</p>



<p>a. The lot is 18,000 square feet or less; and</p>
